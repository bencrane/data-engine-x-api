## Observations

1. **The federal contract leads data is rich and outbound-ready.** Every SAM-matched lead has a named government POC with title, plus company address and phone. 97% of manufacturing first-time awardees have a SAM match.

2. **First-time awardees are the warmest outbound segment.** Companies that just won their first federal contract need help with compliance, scaling, and winning repeat business. There are 43,487 of them.

3. **IT/professional services and manufacturing are the two biggest verticals** for first-time awardees (30% and 23% respectively). Manufacturing has better contact data quality (100% POC coverage vs. IT which may have more sole proprietors).

4. **SBA and federal contracts serve different markets.** SBA loans go to restaurants, gyms, and beauty salons. Federal contracts go to IT firms, manufacturers, and engineering companies. They rarely overlap by NAICS — but when they do (manufacturing), the SBA loan data could signal companies gearing up to bid on federal contracts.

5. **The SAM.gov POC data is the key differentiator.** Unlike typical B2B databases that give you generic info@ emails, SAM.gov gives you the named government business point of contact — the person responsible for federal procurement at that company. Titles like "VP of Government Affairs," "President," and "COO" — these are decision-makers.

6. **No SBA-to-federal join exists yet** because SBA data has no UEI. Fuzzy matching on company name + city + state would unlock a "recently SBA-funded manufacturers" segment that could be cross-referenced against federal contract activity.
